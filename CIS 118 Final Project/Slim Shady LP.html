Slim Shady LP

Released February 23, 1999

Speaking mostly through his alter ego Slim Shady, Eminem deep dives into his own turbulent psyche and domestic life in this album. In its blurring of fantasy and reality, the album provoked accusations of homophobia and glorification of drugs and violence. Produced by proven hip-hop heavyweight Dr. Dre and Detroit’s aspiring Bass Brothers, it was a pop crossover smash and a glimpse of rap brilliance. While it did sell 5.5 million copies in the US, it wasn't a big achievement for Eminem. His big peak will come in the next few albums.
